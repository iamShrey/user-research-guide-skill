# Interview Guide Template

Use this template as the output structure for every guide generated by this skill. Adapt section lengths and optional sections based on the research objective and session length.

---

```

**Research Objective:** [One paragraph statement of what this research is trying to learn and why]

- [SRQ 3]

**Target Participant:** [Role, experience level, relevant behaviors that qualify them]
**Session Length:** [e.g., 60 minutes]
**Format:** [Remote / In-person / Moderated usability / Generative]
**Prepared by:** [Researcher name]
**Date:** [Date]

## Moderator Notes
*[Brief notes for the person running the session: key sensitivities, what to prioritize if time is short, any artifacts or stimuli to have ready]*

## Section 1: Warm-Up (5–10 min)
*Goal: Build rapport and establish the participant's context. Keep it light and conversational. Don't start taking heavy notes yet — just listen.*

1. Can you start by telling me a bit about your role and what a typical day looks like for you?

2. How long have you been doing [relevant role/activity]?

3. [Optional context-setter relevant to the research topic — e.g., "What tools do you rely on most day-to-day?"]

---

## Section 2: Current State / Baseline Behavior (10–15 min)
*Goal: Understand what participants currently do before introducing the research topic. Anchor in specific, recent experiences.*

*[Opening bridge — transition from warm-up to the research topic area. Example: "I want to spend some time understanding how you currently handle [area]. No right or wrong answers here — I'm just trying to understand your real experience."]*

4. Walk me through the last time you [performed the core behavior relevant to the research]. What was the context?

5. What does that process usually look like from start to finish?

6. What tools or resources do you rely on to do that?

   *Probe: Are there any workarounds you've built in? Anything you wish worked differently?*

---

## Section 3: [SRQ 1 Topic] (8–12 min)
*Goal: [State what you're trying to understand in this section and why it matters]*

7. [Primary question — open-ended, behavioral]

   *Probe: [Follow-up to go deeper]*

   *Probe: [Follow-up if they mention something unexpected]*

8. [Secondary question — explore a specific aspect or edge case]

   *[Pause]*

9. "Why does [X they mentioned] matter to you?"

   *Ladder: Keep asking "why does that matter?" until you reach an underlying need.*

---

## Section 4: [SRQ 2 Topic] (8–12 min)
*Goal: [State what you're trying to understand]*

10. [Primary question]

    *Probe: [Follow-up]*

11. Can you give me a specific example of that? Tell me about the last time it happened.

12. [Emotion/impact question — "What was that like for you?" / "What was the impact of that?"]

---

## Section 5: [SRQ 3 Topic] (8–12 min)
*Goal: [State what you're trying to understand]*

13. [Primary question]

    *Probe: [Follow-up]*

14. [Comparative question — e.g., "How does that compare to how you handled this a year ago?" or "How do others on your team approach this differently?"]*

15. [Hypothetical or forward-looking — use sparingly, anchor in real context: "If that were easier to do, what would change for you?"]*

---

## Section 6: Concept / Stimulus Reaction (optional, 10–15 min)
*Only include if you're testing a concept, prototype, or artifact.*
*Instructions: Show the stimulus now. Give the participant 30 seconds to look at it in silence before asking anything.*

16. What's your first reaction to this?

    *[Pause — let them speak fully before following up]*

17. What stands out to you, if anything?

18. Where does this fit — or not fit — with how you currently [do the relevant thing]?

19. What questions does this raise for you?

    *Probe: What would you want to know before using something like this?*

---

## Section 7: Closing (5 min)
*Goal: Catch anything missed, leave the participant feeling heard, and close gracefully.*

20. Is there anything about [topic] that we haven't talked about that you think is important for us to understand?

21. Is there anything you expected me to ask that I didn't?

*[Moderator note: Briefly explain next steps — how the research will be used, what happens to the recording/notes, and when they can expect to hear back if applicable.]*

*Thank the participant warmly. Let them know their input is genuinely valuable and will directly inform [product/decision].*
```
